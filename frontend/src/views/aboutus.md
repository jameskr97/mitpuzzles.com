This website hosts a number of classic logic puzzles, such as Minesweeper and Sudoku, as well as some lesser-known ones, like Light-Up and Tents — we hope you will enjoy them. The creators of this website are part of a multi-institutional collaboration between researchers at MIT, Harvard, NYU, and the University of Edinburgh interested in understanding how people solve complex problems.

Our goal is to uncover how people manage their cognitive resources when facing challenging puzzles. We study decision-making, memory limitations, and strategic learning. The primary aim of this website is to gain insight into how puzzle-solving skills and strategies evolve over time with experience. Every click, move, and guess you make during gameplay provides invaluable data for our research, helping us to understand how people reason about complex problems.

## Privacy & Ethics

We are committed to maintaining your privacy. All collected data is entirely anonymous and strictly for research purposes. Your gameplay data will never be shared commercially or used outside our scientific studies.

## Be a Part of Science

When you play the puzzles on mitpuzzles.com, you’re not just solving challenges—you’re actively contributing to science. By playing the games on this website, you will be helping us advance our understanding of how people solve complex problems.
So, thank you for your contribution to science, and we sincerely hope you have fun!
If you encounter any errors or issues, please let us know by pressing the “Feedback” button and describing the issue in as much detail as you can. If you have any questions or concerns or would otherwise like to get in touch, please send an email to Sam Cheyette (cheyette@mit.edu).
